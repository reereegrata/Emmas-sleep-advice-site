1. Nooie Baby Monitor — Best Budget Smart Monitor

~AU$60 (camera + app, no dedicated screen)

The Nooie baby monitor is our pick for Australian parents who want smart features without spending a fortune. At around AU$60, this camera streams 1080p HD video straight to your phone via the app. It has infrared night vision, two-way audio with noise reduction, and motion tracking that follows your baby automatically. It also monitors room temperature and works with Alexa and Google Assistant. You can save recordings to a MicroSD card or cloud storage starting at AU$1/month. Since it's app-based, you'll need your phone to check on baby.

✅ Pros
  • Very affordable (~AU$60)
  • 1080p HD video with night vision
  • Motion tracking (auto pan/tilt)
  • Two-way audio with noise reduction
  • Temperature sensor
❌ Cons
  • No dedicated screen (phone only)
  • Needs WiFi and the app
  • Basic build at this price point
Who should buy this: Budget-conscious Australian parents who already have a smartphone and want video monitoring without buying a separate screen.

2. Cubo AI Baby Monitor — Best Smart Monitor

~AU$449 (camera + app, no dedicated screen)

The Cubo AI is the smartest baby monitor in our lineup. It uses AI to detect when your baby's face is covered by a blanket, when they roll over onto their stomach, and even when they cough — sending alerts straight to your phone. The overhead mount gives you a full view of the cot with no blind spots. No-glow night vision means no disturbing red LEDs. It has automatic lullaby triggering, the "Moments Wall" for milestone photos, and sleep analytics that track duration, wake events, and caregiver interactions.

✅ Pros
  • AI detects face covering, rollovers, and coughs
  • Overhead mount = no blind spots
  • No-glow night vision
  • Auto lullabies when baby wakes
  • Sleep analytics and milestone photos
  • Danger zone alerts for toddlers
❌ Cons
  • Expensive (~AU$449)
  • Needs WiFi
  • No dedicated screen (phone only)
  • Some parents don't need all the AI features
Who should buy this: Tech-savvy parents who want maximum peace of mind, especially for newborns.

3. Eufy Baby Monitor — Best Video Quality & Privacy

~AU$210-299 (hybrid: screen + optional app)

If privacy and video quality matter most, Eufy is the pick. Select models have a physical WiFi kill switch on the camera — flip it off and the monitor becomes a closed-circuit system with no internet access. The local range reaches up to 1,000 feet without needing WiFi. The parent screen supports split-screen viewing for up to 4 cameras. Active noise reduction filters out background sounds. All video saves locally to a MicroSD card with zero subscription fees. The parent unit battery lasts up to 12 hours.

✅ Pros
  • Physical WiFi kill switch for total privacy
  • 1,000-foot local range
  • Split-screen for up to 4 cameras
  • Active noise reduction
  • No subscription fees
  • 12-hour battery on parent unit
❌ Cons
  • Pricier than basic options
  • Features vary by model
Who should buy this: Privacy-conscious parents who want reliable local monitoring with a dedicated screen and no monthly fees.

4. Lollipop Baby Monitor — Best Design & Flexibility

~AU$239-269 (camera + app, no dedicated screen)

The Lollipop looks nothing like a traditional baby monitor. It's wrapped in soft, toxic-free silicone in pastel colours, with a bendable stem you can wrap around the crib for a true overhead view. The 1080p video streams to your phone via the app. It has true cry detection that filters out background noise. An optional sensor monitors room temperature and humidity. With the premium subscription, it offers non-wearable breathing tracking — the camera tracks the rise and fall of your baby's chest without any sensors on their skin.

✅ Pros
  • Flexible, tool-free mounting
  • 1080p video with true cry detection
  • Non-wearable breathing tracking (subscription)
  • Pastel colours, nursery-friendly
  • Continuous audio mode even with phone locked
❌ Cons
  • No dedicated screen (phone only)
  • Premium features need subscription (AU$60-120/year)
  • Mid-range pricing
Who should buy this: Style-conscious parents who want a flexible, app-based monitor that looks good in the nursery.

5. Uniden Baby Monitor — Best Trusted Brand

~AU$119-149 (video monitor with screen)

Uniden is a name Australian parents know and trust. This video monitor comes with a dedicated parent screen — no phone needed. Select models also offer app access so you can check in remotely. Motorised pan, tilt, and zoom covers 355 degrees horizontally and 60 degrees vertically. Temperature and humidity sensors monitor the nursery climate. Danger zone geofencing lets you draw virtual boundaries — the camera alerts you if your toddler moves into a restricted area. No-glow infrared night vision means no disturbing red lights.

✅ Pros
  • Trusted Australian brand
  • Dedicated parent screen included
  • Optional phone app access
  • Pan/tilt/zoom with 355° coverage
  • Temperature and humidity sensors
  • Danger zone alerts
❌ Cons
  • Mid-range price
  • Some features need the app
Who should buy this: Australian parents who want a dedicated screen from a brand they know, with the option to check in via phone when needed.

6. VTech Baby Monitor — Best Budget Audio

~AU$30-60 (audio only)

Sometimes you just need to hear when your baby cries. The VTech audio baby monitor is simple, reliable, and very affordable. No apps, no screens, no WiFi. It uses DECT technology for clear, interference-free sound. Battery life is excellent — up to 19 hours on a single charge with some models. It has two-way talk so you can soothe your baby from another room.

✅ Pros
  • Most affordable option (~AU$30-60)
  • Excellent battery life (up to 19 hours)
  • Clear DECT sound, no interference
  • Simple and reliable
  • Two-way talk
❌ Cons
  • Audio only (no video)
  • No app or smart features
  • Basic design
Who should buy this: Australian parents on a tight budget, or those who already have a video camera and just want a backup audio monitor. Great for grandparents.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best baby monitor in Australia?+
For most families, the Nooie (~AU$60) is great value. If you want a dedicated screen, the Uniden (~AU$119-149) is a trusted choice. For smart features, go with Cubo AI (~AU$449).
Are WiFi baby monitors safe?+
Modern monitors use encrypted connections. For maximum security, choose a hybrid model like Eufy with a physical WiFi kill switch.
What's the best baby monitor for newborns?+
The Cubo AI is best for newborns thanks to its rollover and face-cover alerts. The Nooie is a reliable budget alternative.
Do I need a baby monitor with video?+
Not necessarily. Many parents start with an audio monitor and upgrade later. But for first-time parents, video is worth the investment.
Where to buy baby monitors in Australia?+
Most are available on Amazon AU. You can also check Baby Bunting for in-store options or Kogan for deals.
