Types of Baby Swaddles — Which One Does Your Baby Need?

Not all swaddles are the same. The right type depends on how your baby likes to sleep:

Arms-Up Swaddles

These let your baby sleep with hands near their face — the natural in-womb position. Best for self-soothers who like to suck on their hands. Our pick: Love to Dream Swaddle Up.

Zip-Up Swaddle Sacks

A wearable sleeping bag with a zip closure. Easy to use — no wrapping skills needed. Many convert to sleep bags by opening the armholes. Our pick: ergoPouch Cocoon.

Velcro Wrap Swaddles

The simplest type — wrap the wings around and secure with velcro. Fast, secure, and the most affordable option. Our pick: SwaddleMe Original.

Muslin Blanket Swaddles

Traditional square blankets — the most versatile but requires wrapping technique. Breathable for summer, multi-use beyond swaddling. Our pick: Aden + Anais Essentials.

Transition Swaddles

Designed for the 3-4 month window when babies start rolling. Removable wings let you free one arm at a time. Our pick: Love to Dream Transition.

Swaddle Safety — What Red Nose Australia Says

Safe swaddling isn't just about getting a good night's sleep — it's about protecting your baby. Red Nose Australia, the country's leading authority on safe sleep and SIDS prevention, has clear guidelines every parent should follow:

Hip-Healthy Swaddling

Your baby's legs should be able to bend up and out at the hips. A swaddle that's too tight around the legs can increase the risk of hip dysplasia. The ergoPouch Cocoon is the only swaddle on this list with official IHDI hip-healthy certification. Look for swaddles with a wide, roomy bottom — not a tight tube shape.

When to Stop Swaddling

Red Nose recommends stopping swaddling as soon as your baby shows any signs of rolling — typically around 3-4 months. Once a swaddled baby can roll onto their tummy, they may not be able to roll back, creating a suffocation risk. Read our full guide on when and how to transition →

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best baby swaddle in Australia?+
For most Australian families, the ergoPouch Cocoon (AU$40-65 depending on TOG) is our top pick — hip-healthy certified, zip-up for easy nappy changes, and available in TOG ratings for every Australian season. The Love to Dream Swaddle Up (AU$45-55) is best for babies who prefer arms-up sleeping.
What TOG swaddle for summer and winter in Australia?+
For Australian summers (room temps 24°C+), use a 0.2 TOG swaddle like the ergoPouch Cocoon 0.2 or a lightweight muslin swaddle. For winter (16-20°C), choose a 2.5 TOG swaddle like the Love to Dream 2.5 TOG or Tommee Tippee Grobag. Read our full TOG & temperature guide →
How do I swaddle my baby safely?+
Red Nose Australia recommends: always place your baby on their back to sleep, keep the swaddle firm but not tight around the hips, and stop swaddling as soon as your baby shows signs of rolling. Never swaddle above the shoulders and ensure the swaddle cannot cover your baby's face. Read our complete how-to guide →
When should I transition out of a swaddle?+
Red Nose Australia advises stopping swaddling as soon as your baby shows any signs of rolling — typically around 3-4 months. Transition swaddles like the Love to Dream Transition let you gradually free one arm at a time. Read our full transition guide →
How many swaddles do I need for a newborn?+
For newborns, we recommend 3-4 swaddles minimum — one to wear, one in the wash, and one spare for middle-of-the-night blowouts. If you're using a swaddle for every sleep, having a spare in each TOG rating (summer + winter) is ideal.
