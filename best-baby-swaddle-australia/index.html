Types of Baby Swaddles Explained

Not all swaddles are the same. The right type depends on how your baby likes to sleep — and the season.

Arms-Up Swaddles

Let babies sleep with hands near their face — the natural in-womb position. Best for self-soothers. Our pick: Love to Dream Swaddle Up (AU$45-50).

Zip-Up Swaddle Sacks

Wearable sleeping bag with a zip closure — no wrapping needed. Most convert to sleep bags once rolling starts. Our pick: ergoPouch Cocoon (AU$43-75) for TOG range, Tommee Tippee Grobag (AU$42-50) for aloe vera fabric.

Velcro Wrap Swaddles

The simplest type — wrap and secure with hook-and-loop tabs in seconds. Most affordable option. Our pick: SwaddleMe Original (AU$20-23).

Muslin Blanket Swaddles

Traditional square wraps — breathable for summer, multi-use beyond swaddling (pram cover, burp cloth, nursing shield). Requires wrapping technique. Our pick: Aden + Anais Essentials 4-pack (AU$50-54).

Transition Swaddles

For the 3-4 month rolling window. Zip-off wings free one arm at a time for a gentle transition. Our pick: Love to Dream Transition (AU$50-65).

Swaddle Safety — What Red Nose Australia Says

Red Nose Australia, the country's leading authority on SIDS prevention, recommends:

  • Always back to sleep — never place a swaddled baby on their tummy or side.
  • Hip-healthy swaddling — baby's legs should bend up and out. The ergoPouch Cocoon and Love to Dream Swaddle Up are both IHDI-certified for hip health.
  • Stop when rolling starts — as soon as your baby shows signs of rolling (typically 3-4 months), stop swaddling immediately.
  • Never above shoulders — the swaddle must sit below the neck and cannot ride up to cover your baby's face.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best baby swaddle in Australia?+
For most Australian families, the ergoPouch Cocoon is the top all-rounder. For babies who prefer the natural arms-up position, the Love to Dream Swaddle Up (AU$45-50) is our pick.
What TOG swaddle for summer and winter in Australia?+
Summer (24°C+): 0.2 TOG. Winter (16-20°C): 2.5 TOG. The Love to Dream Swaddle Up covers both with its Lite (0.2) and Warm (2.5) versions.
How do I swaddle my baby safely?+
Red Nose Australia says: back to sleep always, firm but not tight around hips, stop when rolling starts, never above shoulders. The Love to Dream Swaddle Up is IHDI-certified hip-healthy.
When should I transition out of a swaddle?+
As soon as your baby shows any signs of rolling — around 3-4 months. The Love to Dream Transition Swaddle lets you free one arm at a time.
How many swaddles do I need?+
3-4 minimum for newborns — one to wear, one in the wash, one spare. Having a summer and winter TOG gives you year-round coverage.
