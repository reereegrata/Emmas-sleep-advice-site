Baby Sleep Schedules by Age, Your Complete 0-12 Month Hub

By Emma · Updated July 2026 · 2 min read
Home › Guides › Baby Sleep Schedules by Age

Every baby is different — but having an age-appropriate schedule takes the guesswork out of sleep. We've created five in-depth guides covering every stage from newborn through toddler. Each guide includes hourly sample schedules, wake windows by age, feeding integration, and Red Nose Australia safe sleep guidelines. Pick your baby's age range below.

👶 Newborn

Newborn Sleep Schedule: 0-3 Months

Covers newborn sleep cycles, circadian rhythm development, wake windows from 30-90 minutes, feeding and sleep integration, day/night confusion, and Red Nose safe sleep for the fourth trimester. Includes hourly sample schedules for 0-4 weeks, 4-8 weeks, and 8-12 weeks.

🍼 Early Infancy

Baby Sleep Schedule: 4-6 Months

Everything changes at 4 months — the sleep regression hits, wake windows extend to 1.5-2.5 hours, and many babies start solids. This guide covers the 4-month regression in detail, the 4-to-3 nap transition, sample schedules by month, and how to build a consistent routine through the regression.

🧸 Older Baby

Baby Sleep Schedule: 7-9 Months

The 8-month regression, separation anxiety, teething, crawling, and the 3-to-2 nap transition — this age packs in more sleep disruptors than any other. Includes sample schedules for 7, 8, and 9 months, solids integration, and how to handle early morning waking.

🚼 Almost Toddler

Baby Sleep Schedule: 10-12 Months

The bridge to toddlerhood — 11-month regression, the 2-to-1 nap transition, walking and cruising milestones disrupting sleep, and separation anxiety at its peak. Includes month-by-month sample schedules and how to know when your baby is ready for one nap.

👧 Toddler

Toddler Sleep Guide: 12-36 Months

Welcome to toddlerhood — nap transitions (2-to-1 and dropping the last nap), the complete regression timeline (12mo, 18mo, 2yr, 3yr), crib-to-bed transition, bedtime stall tactics, and toddler-safe sleep guidelines. Covers everything from just-turned-one through the three-year sleep shift.
