Are Baby Monitors Worth It? — An Honest Guide for Australian Parents

By Emma · Updated June 2026 · 5 min read
← See our top baby monitor picks

If you're pregnant or just brought your newborn home, you've probably asked yourself: do I really need a baby monitor? With prices ranging from AU$20 to AU$500, it's a fair question. The short answer: for most Australian parents, a baby monitor is worth it — but not for the reasons you might think.

This guide breaks down the real costs, the genuine benefits, and — just as importantly — the situations where you can comfortably skip the monitor and spend your money elsewhere. Already using one and wondering when to phase it out? Read our guide on when to stop using a baby monitor. Not sure what type you need? Start with our complete baby monitor buying guide.

Do You Actually Need a Baby Monitor?

Let's be honest: no, you don't need one in the same way you need a safe cot and a firm mattress. Red Nose Australia doesn't list baby monitors as essential safety equipment. Parents raised babies for centuries without them.

But here's what changes everything: modern parenting is different. Most Australian homes are designed with the nursery separate from the living areas. You're not spending all day within earshot of your baby like previous generations did. A monitor bridges that gap.

The real question isn't "do I need one?" It's "will this reduce my anxiety and improve my quality of life?" For most parents, the answer is a clear yes. See our 6 Best Baby Monitors comparison to find one that fits your budget.

What a Baby Monitor Costs in Australia — Real Numbers

Baby monitors in Australia range from under AU$20 for a basic audio unit to over AU$500 for a premium smart monitor with AI features. Here's what you actually get at each price point:

Audio Monitors (~AU$20-AU$60)

Basic sound-only monitoring. The VTech audio monitor (~AU$30-40) is the most popular choice — simple, reliable, and does exactly what it says. You'll hear your baby cry, but you won't know if they're just resettling or if they've kicked off their blanket. For budget-conscious parents or as a travel backup, this is all you need.

Video Monitors (~AU$60-AU$200)

This is the sweet spot for most Australian families. For around AU$60-150, you get a camera with night vision, two-way talk, and a clear picture. The Nooie baby monitor (~AU$60) is our top budget pick, while the Uniden (~AU$119-149) gives you a dedicated screen instead of relying on your phone. At this price, the upgrade from audio to video is absolutely worth it.

Smart/WiFi Monitors (~AU$200-AU$500)

Premium features like AI rollover detection, sleep tracking, and the ability to check from anywhere. The Cubo AI (~AU$449) and Eufy (~AU$210-299) lead this category. Worth it if you're an anxious first-time parent or you travel for work and want to check in remotely. Overkill if you just want to hear when your baby wakes up.

5 Reasons a Baby Monitor Is Worth Every Dollar

1. Peace of Mind You Can't Put a Price On

This is the #1 reason parents buy monitors. That little screen lets you check on your baby without opening the door and potentially waking them. For anxious parents — and most first-timers are — it's genuinely life-changing. You'll stop tiptoeing to the nursery every 20 minutes just to check they're still breathing.

2. Better Sleep for Everyone (Yes, You Too)

Without a monitor, every little noise from the nursery triggers a full mission: get up, walk down the hall, open the door, check, close the door, walk back, lie down, repeat. With a monitor, one glance tells you "they're just resettling" — and you stay in bed. Over a year, that's hundreds of hours of extra sleep.

3. You'll Catch Problems Before They Escalate

A monitor with temperature sensors helps you keep the nursery in the safe 18-22°C range Red Nose Australia recommends. You'll notice if your toddler has climbed out of the cot before they wander into the hallway. You'll hear a cough or cry pattern that tells you something's wrong before it becomes a full-blown emergency.

4. Freedom to Move Around Your Home

Without a monitor, you're tethered to within earshot of the nursery. With one, you can be in the backyard hanging washing, in the garage, or watching TV in the lounge room — and still know exactly what's happening with your baby. For Australian homes where living areas are often at the opposite end of the house, this is a practical game-changer.

5. It Grows With Your Child — Not Just for Newborns

A good monitor isn't just for the newborn phase. Toddlers need monitoring too — especially during transitions like moving to a big bed or night-time potty training. A monitor with two-way talk lets you tell your toddler "stay in bed" without going in, which helps them learn to self-settle. Your AU$100 investment can easily last 2-3 years.

When You DON'T Need a Baby Monitor

Monitors aren't for everyone. Here are the situations where you can confidently skip it:

Your Home Is Small Enough to Hear Everything

If you live in a one-bedroom apartment or a small single-storey house where you can hear your baby from every room, a monitor adds little value. Save your money — you've got built-in audio monitoring.

Your Baby Is Still Sleeping in Your Room

Red Nose Australia recommends room-sharing for the first 6-12 months. If your baby is in a cot next to your bed, you don't need a monitor yet. Wait until they transition to their own room — that's when it becomes genuinely useful.

You're on a Tight Budget and Can Wait

A safe cot, a firm mattress, and fitted sheets come first. If buying a monitor means compromising on these essentials, wait. You can always add a monitor later when finances allow. A basic audio monitor at AU$20-30 is also a perfectly fine stopgap.

Video vs Audio Monitor — Which One Is Worth the Extra Money?

If you can afford the jump from audio (~AU$30) to video (~AU$60), do it. The AU$30 difference is tiny compared to the benefit of seeing whether your baby is actually awake or just making noise in their sleep. Without video, every grunt sends you into the nursery. With video, one glance tells you everything.

Audio-only makes sense if: you're experienced (second or third child), you only need to know when the baby is really crying, or you're buying a backup monitor for travel or grandparents' house.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are baby monitors actually worth the money?

For most Australian parents, yes — especially in the first 12 months. A basic video monitor costs ~AU$60-150 and gives you peace of mind, better sleep, and the ability to check on your baby without disturbing them. If you're on a tight budget, an audio-only monitor at AU$20-40 is a solid alternative.

Do I really need a baby monitor as a first-time parent?

You don't absolutely need one, but most first-time parents find a baby monitor invaluable. The anxiety of not knowing what's happening in the nursery is real, and a monitor gives you the confidence to step away — whether it's to shower, cook, or just sit on the couch.

What's the cheapest baby monitor that's still worth buying in Australia?

A basic audio monitor like the VTech (~AU$20-40) is the cheapest option that's still reliable. For video, the Nooie baby monitor at around AU$60 offers great value — 1080p video, night vision, and two-way talk at an entry-level price.

Is a video baby monitor worth the extra cost over audio?

Yes, for most parents. The price difference between a basic audio monitor (~AU$30) and an entry-level video monitor (~AU$60) is small, but the benefit is significant. Being able to see if your baby is actually awake or just resettling saves you from unnecessary trips into the nursery.

When is a baby monitor NOT worth it?

A baby monitor may not be worth it if you live in a small apartment where you can hear everything, if your baby is still sleeping in your room (recommended for the first 6-12 months by Red Nose Australia), or if you're on a very tight budget and need to prioritise other essentials like a safe cot and mattress first.
