White Noise Safety — What Red Nose Australia Says

A 2014 study in Pediatrics tested 14 infant sleep machines and found all of them could produce sound above 50 decibels when placed close to the cot. At maximum volume and close range, three exceeded 85 dB — heavy city traffic levels.

Red Nose Australia and the AAP recommend:

  • Keep it at 50 decibels or below — about the volume of a quiet conversation.
  • Place it at least 2 metres from the cot — never on the cot rail, headboard, or mattress.
  • Never inside or attached to the cot — the cot is for your baby and a fitted sheet only.
  • Test with a free decibel app — Decibel X or NIOSH SLM, both free. Place your phone where baby's head would be.
  • A timer is fine — no evidence white noise needs to play all night. A 30–60 minute timer covering the falling-asleep window works.

Quick test: Stand next to the cot with the machine running. If you can hold a normal conversation without raising your voice, the volume is likely safe. If you have to speak up, turn it down. Read our full decibel safety guide →

Frequently Asked Questions

How loud should a baby white noise machine be?+
Red Nose Australia and the AAP recommend 50 decibels or below at the cot. Place the machine at least 2 metres from your baby's cot. Download a free decibel meter app (Decibel X or NIOSH SLM) to check your setup.
What is the best white noise machine for babies in Australia?+
For most families, the Hatch Rest 2 (AU$105-130) is our top pick — app-controlled, 11 loop-free sounds, night light with LED clock, and toddler OK-to-wake feature. For budget, the Dreamegg D1 Classic (AU$45-55) has 24 sounds. For portability, the ergoPouch Drift Away (AU$52-55) has a 48-hour battery.
Does white noise help babies sleep?+
Yes. A 2020 study found 80% of newborns fell asleep within 5 minutes with white noise vs only 25% without. White noise mimics the constant whooshing sound babies heard in the womb and masks sudden household noises like doorbells or barking dogs.
Can white noise damage my baby's hearing?+
Not when used correctly. Follow the 50 dB / 2-metre rule. A 2014 Pediatrics study found all 14 machines tested could exceed safe levels at close range — which is why placement and volume control matter. Never place the machine in or on the cot.
Portable vs nursery white noise machine — which do I need?+
A portable machine (Welcare Sleep-Tight, ergoPouch Drift Away, Baby Shusher) is best for travel, prams, or moving between rooms. A nursery machine (Hatch Rest 2, Glow Sleep Easy, Dreamegg D1 Classic) is better as a permanent setup with extra features. Many families buy one of each.
